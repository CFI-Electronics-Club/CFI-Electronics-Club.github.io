EC01: MAARG - A Personalized, Location-Aware Audio Guide for Museums

Ever found it to navigate yourself through a closed indoor space? In order to achieve this, it becomes important to know the exact location of the individual indoors. We carry out the Wifi FTM(fine time measurement) procedure to obtain ranges and locate the individual. This position data is further pushed into an edge server where it is stored and processed such that the web server interacts with the individual, providing him personalized experience of navigating through the indoor space

EC03: Smart Tripod

Here's an automated mobile tripod which can track your face and adjust its position accordingly to get the best shot! The Raspberry Pi camera detects faces and uses UDP protocol to send the coordinates to an ESP32 interfacing two motors. A servo motor adjusting the camera's vertical orientation A DC motor controlling mecanum wheels giving mobilty to the tripod.

EC04: VR Haptic Glove

Our project aims to allow users to realistically "feel" and "touch" virtual reality objects, thus making the entire VR experience more realistic. How does it work? The thermal feedback is implemented through peltier devices, which provide heating or cooling effect based on the current flowing through it. We also have servo motors to provide force feedback by restricting the movement of fingers while "holding" VR objects.

EC05: EMG Sensor

Our Sensor Captures Electromyography Signals from human muscles for Analysis of Fatigue, Stamina, Movement and Many More! The Sensor collects the Electromyogram signals from the muscles which is then filtered and amplified. The amplified data is wirelessly sent for further computing on an Edge device! The data is then processed using signal processing techniques and then analyzed for fatigue, stamina and many other characteristics of the muscle!
